## 1. MALWARE & ONLINE DANGERS

| Termine | Definizione |
|---|---|
| **Virus** | A program that infiltrates a computer and then causes it to malfunction. |
| **Spyware** | A secret program that observes and reports on a user's online behaviour without their knowledge, usually for commercial purposes. May also display pop-up ads. |
| **Spam** | Unwanted e-mail messages transmitted to mass audiences, often for advertising. Usually harmless, but can carry viruses. |
| **Worm** | A program that invades computers on a network and hides deep inside the software. It replicates itself to prevent removal and can interfere with operations. |
| **Trojan** | A program that enters a computer by hiding inside an apparently innocent application. Once inside, it may steal confidential information like passwords and credit card details. |
| **Botnet** | A large group of computers under the control of a criminal gang, infected without the owners' knowledge, used for criminal purposes. |
| **Phishing** | A method of tricking people into revealing sensitive personal information (bank details, passwords), usually via an e-mail that looks like a genuine message. |

## 2. CYBERCRIME GLOSSARY

### Baiting
**EN:** A provocative act used to solicit an angry, aggressive or emotional response from another individual.  
**IT:** Può configurare reati di cyberstalking (art. 612-bis c.p.) → 6 mesi–5 anni; diffamazione (art. 595 c.p.) → 6 mesi–3 anni se commessa online.

### Flaming
**EN:** The act of posting insults, often including profanity, on the Internet. Emerges from the anonymity that online forums provide.  
**IT:** Rientra nella diffamazione (art. 595 c.p.). Pene: fino a 1 anno o multa €1.032; se commesso via social/web → 6 mesi–3 anni o multa min. €516.

### Catfish
**EN:** Someone who creates a fake online profile to deceive others, typically for romantic scams, financial fraud or personal amusement (stolen images + false info).  
**IT:** Non sempre reato, ma diventa penalmente perseguibile se finalizzato a truffa/estorsione. Reati: sostituzione di persona (art. 494 c.p.), accesso abusivo a sistema informatico (art. 615-ter c.p.) → fino a 3 anni.

### Grooming
**EN:** Behaviour used to establish an emotional connection with a vulnerable person under the age of consent, with the objective of sexual abuse.  
**IT:** Art. 609-undecies c.p. – adescamento di minorenni → 1–3 anni di reclusione.

### Neknominate
**EN:** A dangerous viral drinking game where participants film themselves chugging alcohol, post it online, and nominate others to do the same within 24 hours.  
**IT:** Non è un reato specifico, ma può configurare illeciti penali o amministrativi se collegato a comportamenti pericolosi.

### Spoofing
**EN:** A cyberattack where criminals impersonate a trusted source (email address, phone number, website, IP address) to deceive people into revealing information, transferring money, or installing malware.  
**IT:** Punito come frode informatica (art. 640-ter c.p.) → 6 mesi–3 anni. Se con trasferimento di denaro → 1–5 anni. Furto di identità digitale → 2–6 anni.

### Deepfake / Deepfake Abuse
**EN:** A video/image in which a person's face, body or voice has been digitally altered to appear as someone else. *Deepfake abuse* = sharing intimate imagery (real or AI-generated) without consent.  
**IT:** Legge n. 132/2025 – art. 612-quater c.p. → reclusione 1–5 anni per diffusione illecita di contenuti generati/manipolati con IA.

### Digital Forgery
**EN:** The unauthorized manipulation or fabrication of digital content (images, videos, audio, documents) to deceive or misrepresent reality, often using AI.

## 3. SOCIAL & ETHICAL PROBLEMS OF IT

### Digital Divide
The split in society between those who are technology literate and can access digital tools, and those who lack access. Partly economic (technology costs money), partly related to age and education. Leads to growing inequality.

### Information Overload
There is an enormous, constantly growing amount of information on the Internet. Even with efficient search engines, it is difficult to know what is reliable and what is misleading or false.

### Interpersonal Relations
New methods of communication have changed how people relate to each other – business, friendships, romance. Social media is essential for millions but can paradoxically reduce direct human contact. Being "on call 24/7" creates work stress.

### Censorship
Debate between total freedom of expression online vs. controlling content deemed immoral or harmful. Question: who decides what is acceptable? How can foreign websites be regulated?

### Surveillance
Phones and computers leave digital records of exactly when, how and where they are used. The smartphone is the most sophisticated tracking device ever invented. Governments, businesses and criminals can access huge amounts of personal data. Issues: commercial profiling, privacy rights, security services accessing private communications.

### Erosion of Cultural Industries
Digital technology allows perfect copies of music, films, books to be distributed at almost no cost. This devastated the music and film industry (CDs/DVDs sales crashed). Newspapers, magazines and books are also being undermined by free online content. Piracy is widespread and doesn't "feel like stealing" but deprives creators of income.

### Job Displacement
Robots and automation are replacing manual production work in factories. The same is now happening in service industries.

## 4. IT AND THE LAW

| Area | What it covers |
|---|---|
| **Copyright** | Right granted to artists, authors, software writers and creators. Prohibits unauthorised copying/distribution of creative works. |
| **Patent protection** | The inventor of an innovative piece of technology registers it and gets exclusive rights to exploit it commercially for a fixed period. |
| **Data protection** | Laws regulate how organisations collect, store and process personal data. People have the right to see data held about them. |
| **Defamation** | Making a false statement that damages someone's reputation. Written = libel; spoken = slander. Online defamation occurs on social media, blogs, chatrooms. |
| **Illegal content** | Laws prohibit storing/distributing material related to terrorism, hate crimes, child pornography and sexual abuse. |
| **Cybercrime** | Laws combat: hacking, theft of classified information, fraud, identity theft, spreading malware, harassment, cyberbullying, cyberstalking. |

**Key vocabulary:**
- **Hacking** – entering a protected computer system without permission
- **Intellectual property** – creative work legally protected by patent or copyright
- **Stalking** – following/harassing someone persistently
- **Fraud** – gaining money by deliberate deception

## 5. THE WEB & WEB 2.0

**Web 2.0** = the shift from users as passive consumers to active participants in websites and applications.

### Main uses of the web today:
- **Information sharing** – Wikipedia, WikiLeaks, travel wikis; info freely contributed and constantly updated
- **Social networking** – Facebook, MySpace; personal pages to keep in contact, share messages/music/videos
- **Blogging** – personal online diary, regularly updated; includes links, photos, audio/video; interactive (comments). Twitter = shorter, informal blog. Forum = discussion site for people sharing interests.
- **Shopping** – Amazon, eBay; comparison sites; online auctions
- **Virtual worlds & gaming** – Second Life (avatar-based); social gaming (Zynga); online casinos
- **Entertainment sharing** – streaming (Spotify, YouTube); uploading user-generated video; some sites operate illegally via peer-to-peer file sharing

## 6. E-COMMERCE

**Definition:** The buying and selling of products and services through the Internet, via email, the web, direct computer-to-computer communications or smartphone apps.

**Payment** is usually *electronic* (no physical cash/cheque). Credit cards and smart cards are common.

**Key distinction:** A transaction is e-commerce only if it is **automated**. If a human takes the instruction and types it manually, it is NOT e-commerce.

### Pros & Cons

| For businesses | |
|---|---|
| ✅ Lower costs (machines process orders) | ❌ Expensive to set up |
| ✅ Faster transactions, 24/7 availability | ❌ Staff retraining required |
| ✅ Wider/global market reach | ❌ Vulnerable to fraud |
| ✅ Better customer data tracking | ❌ Marketing costs for the website |

| For consumers | |
|---|---|
| ✅ Easy, fast, from home | ❌ Can't physically inspect products |
| ✅ Enormous range of goods | ❌ Fear of fraud / sharing card details |
| ✅ Often lower prices | ❌ Must wait for delivery |
| ✅ Easy price comparison | ❌ Returns can be problematic |

## 7. INTERNET SAFETY – 4 ESSENTIALS

1. **Use a firewall** – software/hardware that creates a protective barrier; blocks unwanted traffic, helps prevent hackers from accessing your computer.

2. **Keep software up to date** – install security updates from manufacturers (Windows, Adobe, browsers). Criminals exploit older versions.

3. **Use protection programs** – anti-virus software scans emails and files for viruses, worms, trojans. If found, isolates or deletes them. Anti-spyware monitors for spyware. Update automatically. *Never* install anti-virus by clicking a pop-up ad.

4. **Take special care with e-mail:**
   - Don't open attachments from unknown senders
   - Don't click unknown hyperlinks (common phishing/spyware tactic)
   - Install a spam filter
   - Never reply to spam (even to "unsubscribe") – it confirms your address is active
